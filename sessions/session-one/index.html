The Unseen Workflow · 5 phases
You already know how to make slides. We're here to fix what AI breaks.
01

Research & Ingestion NotebookLM

Turning chaos into context

Dumping a 100-page PDF into ChatGPT usually results in hallucinated summaries or generic fluff. The real art is in grounding the AI.

Unseen problem: AI loses nuance and contradictions.
Fix: Use NotebookLM to upload sources, then force it to highlight conflicting data points or unexpected outliers. This turns research into active discovery.
02

Narrative Arc Claude / ChatGPT

Fighting the algorithm's formula

AI-generated stories are painfully predictable (Problem → Solution → Conclusion). Audiences tune out.

Unseen problem: AI thinks in features, not in human conflict.
Fix: You define the "Why." Use AI to brainstorm the "What" and "How." Prompt it to structure the narrative around a specific tension or trade-off your audience cares about.
03

Structuring Logic Any LLM

Breaking the robotic symmetry

AI outlines are notoriously symmetrical (Intro, Feature 1, Feature 2, Conclusion). This creates boring, predictable slides.

Unseen problem: AI organizes by list, not by priority.
Fix: Force the AI to think in terms of audience benefits. Ask it: "What does the audience need to hear *first* to care about the rest?" Build the pyramid from there.
04

Writing with Style Claude

Killing the AI voice

Everyone can spot AI writing a mile away—too many words, too formal, zero personality. You need an edge.

Unseen problem: AI writes like a corporate memo.
Fix: Use Claude for nuanced rewrites. Feed it examples of writers you admire (e.g., "Rewrite this slide in the concise, punchy style of Steve Jobs"). This is where style separates the good from the great.
05

Design Translation Gamma / Midjourney

Bridging writing and visuals

Great writers are rarely great designers, and vice versa. AI doesn't bridge this gap—it widens it.

Unseen problem: Text-heavy slides kill presentations.
Fix: Separate the concerns. Use Gamma or Beautiful.ai to handle the visual heavy lifting, and use DALL-E/Midjourney for custom, concept-driven imagery. Let the writer write, and let the designer design—even if both are AI tools.
